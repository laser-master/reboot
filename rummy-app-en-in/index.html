How to Play Rummy: A Complete Guide

Step 1: Understand the Basics

Rummy is a game where you need to arrange cards into valid sequences and sets. A sequence is three or more cards in consecutive order of the same suit, while a set is three or more cards of the same rank but different suits.

Step 2: Game Begins

Each player receives 13 cards, and a random card is selected as the "open card." This open card starts the discard pile. The remaining cards form the stock pile.

Step 3: Your Turn

When it's your turn, you can either draw a card from the stock pile or pick the top card from the discard pile. You must then discard one card to the discard pile.

Step 4: Form Sequences and Sets

Arrange your cards into valid sequences and sets. You need at least one pure sequence (without using a joker) to make a valid declaration.

Step 5: Declare

Once you've arranged all 13 cards into valid sequences and sets, click the "Declare" button. Your hand is then verified, and if valid, you win the round.
